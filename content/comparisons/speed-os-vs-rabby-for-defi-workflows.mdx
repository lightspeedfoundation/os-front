**Rabby is built to make each transaction preview clearer before you sign. Speed OS is built to make *the whole DeFi session* more legible: a single new-tab shell where you can run the same **tooling model** as the desktop CLI, with panels and an optional terminal—while still keeping your signing material in an on-device vault.**

**At a glance**

- **Rabby strength:** per-transaction clarity, address awareness, and an excellent **wallet-shaped** experience for EVM users.
- **Speed OS strength:** a **workspace-shaped** experience for people who want **command composition** (swap / bridge / read / automation hooks) to live next to the rest of the browser.
- **Workflow difference:** Rabby is often the signing surface *around* the dapp. Speed OS tries to be the *home surface* for repeated operations.

**When Rabby is the better fit**  
You jump between dapps, you want a wallet UI that is always in the extension tray, and you do not need a standing **new tab** home for DeFi.

**When Speed OS is the better fit**  
You use **many** small operations per day, you like **Cmd/Ctrl+K** style entry, and you want **one** environment that does not require you to keep re-orienting a new website for every subtask.

**Caveat**  
This page compares **workflows**, not a claim that one product is “more secure” in all threat models. Security depends on your device, update cadence, and what you sign.

**FAQ**

**Do I have to pick one?**  
No. In practice, users may keep a primary wallet and add a workspace. What matters is *where* you store private material and *what* you sign.

**Does Speed OS replace portfolio tracking in Rabby?**  
Not automatically. Treat portfolio sources as a separate question: which chain data, which accounts, and what you trust to be fresh.
